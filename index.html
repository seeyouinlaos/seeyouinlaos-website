The Story

One journey, three days, and the people we love.

Instead of a single evening, we are inviting you into a journey: three days at AVANI+ Luang Prabang, a sunset cruise on the Mekong, and a wedding held as the sun goes down over northern Laos.

Laos is not the backdrop. It is the heart of it. Alms given at dawn, temples and the old town, the warmth of Lao hospitality, dinner under the red lanterns of Manda de Laos.

The Dress Guide

What to wear,
day by day.

Four looks across the weekend, down to the fabric and the shoes, so packing feels easy and nothing is left to guess. One gentle note that matters here: full white belongs to the Sunday dawn Alms Giving, where it is the traditional, respectful colour; for the ceremony and dinner we step away from white, which the bride wears alone. Late February is warm and dry by day, cool at dawn and after dark, so think breathable layers.

The one firm rule

Sunday is black tie.

For the ceremony and the wedding dinner, gentlemen wear a tuxedo or smoking jacket, ladies an evening gown. It is one continuous look, with no outfit change between the two. The ceremony is outdoors and poolside at sunset, so lightweight formal fabrics are your friend. Anything you are unsure about, your Guest Relations team is happy to advise.

Please avoid: pure white and ivory, reserved for the bride, and bright colours or heavy metallics, which pull against the palette.

Your stay

A room that is part
of the journey.

Your Deluxe Room at AVANI+ Luang Prabang opens onto the garden: 35 m², a private terrace or balcony with room for a day bed, a coffee & tea maker for slow mornings, and breakfast included on all days.

The hotel sits in the heart of the UNESCO-listed old town; temples, cafés and the night market are all a short walk away, and the pool is never more than a minute from your door.

Your room is arranged as part of the wedding block. Nothing to book, nothing to pay at check-in. You are our guest.

27 Feb – 1 Mar 2027 · 2 nights · breakfast included
Setthathirath Road, Luang Prabang
The centre of it all

By day a pool.
At sunset, the ceremony.

The dark-tiled pool in the courtyard is where the weekend breathes: morning swims, slow afternoons under the umbrellas, the architecture reflected in still water.

On Sunday it becomes the ceremony. Paddy & Ju welcome you personally and show you to your seat; there are warm cotton towels, a string-quartet prelude, and then, just as golden hour reaches the courtyard, we say yes. Your exact arrival time and seat arrive in your Guest Area closer to the day.

The wedding dinner

Dinner beside
the lily pond.

Manda de Laos is a family home turned restaurant, built around a UNESCO-classified lily pond and the recipes of "Maman Phiew", cooked here since 2015. Red lanterns, warm light, water everywhere.

We take over the pond-side tables for the evening: the Namkong tasting menu, eight courses of Laotian family cuisine, with a curated selection of drinks included through the evening. Every guest is personally seated; your place will be waiting. A string quartet plays us in, and later the cake is cut.

It is a short transfer from AVANI+; vans run for you in both directions, no outfit change needed.
