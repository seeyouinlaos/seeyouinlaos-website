# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Invited wedding guests of Haruthai Amphai and Suthep Thongantang, traveling to Luang Prabang, Laos for the wedding weekend of Sunday 28 February 2027. Most arrive internationally (Europe and Thailand), so their real context is not just "read about a wedding" but "decide to attend, then plan a genuine overseas trip": flights, dates, budget, dress, and what the days hold. The site speaks to these guests. The couple act as hosts and stewards behind it, maintaining content and operations, but they are not the audience; every screen addresses the guest.

## Product Purpose

A luxury guest experience for the See You In Laos wedding that presents the celebration as a shared journey rather than a single event, and removes the friction of attending, especially the hardest part: getting there. In an unhurried editorial voice it communicates the weekend (the moments, the order of the days, dress, places) and it gives guests a working tool to find and track the best real flights for their own dates. Success is a guest who feels the trip is effortless and worth taking, and commits to coming.

## Positioning

A wedding presented as a shared journey along the Mekong, where the practical reality of getting there is treated with the same care as the celebration itself. Not an events page with logistics bolted on, but one continuous guest experience from the first daydream to the last slow goodbye.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: plan and track flights via the Flight Tracker. International travel is the single largest barrier to attending, so the site's primary job is to make that barrier small and legible with real fares for the guest's own dates.
- Secondary CTA: say yes (RSVP by email or LINE via the "How to say yes" steps), and explore the weekend for guests not yet ready to look at flights.
- The line a visitor remembers after ten seconds: Travel first. Wedding second. Memory always, in Luang Prabang, Laos, on 28 February 2027.
- Belief ladder: first, this is a real and beautiful place genuinely worth the journey; then, the weekend is thoughtfully arranged and I will be looked after; then, getting there is manageable because I can see and track the exact flights and fares; finally, I want to be part of this, so I commit and book.
- Proof on hand: real venue and location photography (AVANI+ Luang Prabang, Manda de Laos, the Mekong), the couple's own brand system, and live flight data through Duffel once credentials are configured. No testimonials, by design; this is a private celebration, and trust is earned through concrete, real detail rather than social proof.

## Brand Personality

Quiet luxury, editorial and unhurried, warm but restrained. In three words: serene, considered, generous. The voice is first-person plural from the hosts, calm and hospitable, promising care without announcing it ("Nothing to plan; every transition is arranged for you"). It never sells, shouts, or pads. Prose uses real grammar rather than AI tells, keeps captions and hashtags sparse, and lets real places and real dates carry the feeling. The emotions to evoke, in order, are calm, confidence, anticipation, and the sense of being personally looked after.

## Anti-references

Generic AI or template wedding sites: cookie-cutter theme-builder weddings, stock florals and script-font clichés, the default AI/SaaS look that could belong to any couple. The tell to avoid is anything that reads as assembled from a template rather than made for this specific journey.

## Design Principles

Effortless attendance: remove friction, above all travel friction; every logistic is either arranged or made trackable, so the guest's only real jobs are to say yes and show up.

The journey is the event: present the weekend as one shared, unhurried arc rather than a schedule to execute; favor orientation and calm over urgency and countdowns.

Quiet luxury with real grammar: restraint over decoration, genuine editorial writing over marketing copy, care shown through detail rather than declared in adjectives.

Show, don't tell: real places, real dates, real fares, real dress; concrete specifics earn trust that adjectives cannot.

Answer, don't defer: a guest can find when, where, what, and what to wear, and can see the exact flights, without having to ask; the site does the work rather than pointing elsewhere.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text and placeholders meet contrast minimums against tinted backgrounds; keyboard focus is always visible; the scroll-driven and GSAP motion has a full reduced-motion alternative (crossfade or instant, never a blank section); markup is semantic with labelled controls and image alt text. These are already largely implemented across the guest site and the Flight Tracker and are a release requirement, not a nicety.
